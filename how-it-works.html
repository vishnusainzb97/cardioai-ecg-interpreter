üß¨ Deep Learning: The Brain of Our AI

Understanding how computers learn to "see" like doctors

üåä What is Deep Learning?

Deep Learning is a type of AI that's inspired by the human brain. Just like your brain has billions of neurons connected together, deep learning uses artificial "neurons" organized in layers ‚Äî that's why it's called "deep"!

Each layer learns something different. The first layers learn simple things (like edges and lines), while deeper layers learn complex patterns (like the shape of a heartbeat). By stacking many layers, the AI can understand incredibly complex patterns.

üí° Simple Analogy

Imagine learning to read. First, you learn the alphabet (simple shapes). Then you learn words (combining letters). Then sentences (combining words). Finally, you understand entire stories. Deep learning works the same way ‚Äî learning simple things first, then combining them to understand complex patterns!

‚ö° How Does One Neuron Work?

Each artificial neuron is like a tiny decision-maker. It takes in several numbers (inputs), does some math, and produces one number (output).

Here's what happens inside:

  1. Receives multiple inputs (numbers from ECG data)
  2. Multiplies each by a "weight" (how important is this?)
  3. Adds them all together
  4. Applies an "activation function" (to decide: fire or not?)
  5. Sends the result to the next layer
üí° Simple Analogy

It's like voting! Each input "votes" for what it thinks. The neuron counts all votes, weighs some more heavily than others, and makes a final decision.
